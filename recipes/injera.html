Injera Recipe

Description

Injera is a traditional Ethiopian and Eritrean flatbread with a unique, slightly spongy texture. Made primarily from teff flour, a gluten-free grain native to the region, injera is both a staple food and an essential part of the dining experience in these cultures.

Ingredients

All you need to make traditional Injera is:

  • teff flour (or some plain flour or rice flour)
  • water
  • salt
  • vegetable oil or ghee for cooking

Steps

Steps to Make Injera:

    Mix the Batter:
  1. Combine teff flour and water in a large bowl to form a smooth batter.
  2. Optionally, add a small amount of starter culture to aid fermentation.
    3. Ferment the Batter:
  3. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let the batter ferment at room temperature for 2 to 3 days.
  4. Stir the batter occasionally. The batter should become slightly bubbly and develop a mildly sour aroma.
    5. Prepare for Cooking:
  5. Once fermented, stir the batter and add a pinch of salt.
  6. Adjust the consistency by adding water if necessary. The batter should be pourable but not too thin.
    7. Heat the Pan:
  7. Preheat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
  8. Lightly grease the skillet with a small amount of oil or wipe it with a paper towel dipped in oil.
    9. Cook the Injera:
  9. Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the hot skillet, then quickly tilt the skillet to spread the batter evenly into a thin, round layer.
  10. Cook the injera until bubbles form on the surface and the edges begin to lift from the pan. This typically takes about 1-2 minutes.
  11. Do not flip the injera; it cooks only on one side. When the top surface is set and the edges start to lift, remove the injera from the pan.
    12. Cool and Serve:
  12. Transfer the cooked injera to a clean cloth or plate to cool.
  13. Repeat the cooking process with the remaining batter.
  14. Serve the injera with a variety of Ethiopian or Eritrean dishes, using it to scoop up stews, vegetables, and other accompaniments.
